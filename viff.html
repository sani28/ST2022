Vancouver International Film Festival

Intro

In this project, we worked with the VIFF's Digital Communications Coordinator - Georgina Chaplin, to asses, research, iterate and propose a digital design solution through design thinking practices. Initially researching the organization through an objective approach - we were able to scope out three domain areas that we could look into improving or innovating in. Throughout the following 3 months we iterated through 2 design workshops (1 with VIFF members, 1 participatory), 6 desk crits, and many class discussions to synthesize the depth of our research that we felt best served the need of the brand and organization.

Loop

Loop provides an efficient method of discovering films at VIFF by using AI and curation technology. Based off one's schedule and availability, Loop enables young adults to experience a film with their friends by filtering their preferences and automatically findinng mutual time slots between their friends.

Browsing

By being able to quickly skim content with trailers, summaries and tags - we allow for faster content browsing and the ability to find a film of interest. A CTA for ticket purchasing and the film's theatre location is readily avilable in each film card as well to aid in the information overload between VIFF's website and their festival pamphlet. We also included a watchlist feature to group films and save them for later.

Schedule Sync

Adding an email to an account allows a timetable sync to block out spots that would not work for a user. By syncing, it makes it easier to compare watchlists films and the free slots available between groups of friends.

Polls

Once a user has been able to find a mutual group availability between friends, a voting system was added to help solve decision making between films.
